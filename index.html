Lukas

Investigating brains and machines

MSc student in IT and Cognition

Cognitive Neuroscience and Natural Language Processing

I am a MSc student in the shared fields of Computer Science and Cognition at the University of Copenhagen. I am conducting research in the intersection of Cognitive Neuroscience and Natural Language Processing under the supervision of Isabelle Augenstein, Johannes Bjerva, and Maria Barrett . Previously, I was an undergraduate student in Psychology at the University of Vienna. There, I was working as a Student Research Assistant in the field of Cognitive Psychology in the groups of Ulrich Ansorge and Claus Lamm. During this time, I've also interned in the Clinical Neuropsychology research group at LMU Munich.

My main interests lie in cognitively inspired NLP, a field in which I endeavour to pursue a PhD. Thus, I revel in analysing cognitive data, automate computations, plotting heatmaps, writing regex, and teaching machines to understand human language. Mostly, however, I am debugging, using print statements, and reflecting on why things are not working. Have a look at my CV, the projects or publication section for more information about my work and interests.

The areas I'm interested in

Brains

Mostly frontal, but also temporal

The human brain has caught my attention ever since I was wondering about the diversity of human behaviour. Nothing, in my view, is more exciting than investigating this complex organ. I am mostly interested in the cognitive and computational sides of the neurosciences. I deem vision and language particularly intriguing.

Machines

Mostly maths, but also language

What I've always incredibly enjoyed is solving conundrums. When teaching machines to learn (whatever that may be), it's nothing more or less than trying to solve a conundrum. I mostly revel in teaching machines to understand human language. Perhaps, because I really like reading and writing myself.
