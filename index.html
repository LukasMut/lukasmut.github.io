Lukas

Investigating brains and machines

MSc student in IT and Cognition

Cognitive Neuroscience and Natural Language Processing

I am a MSc student in the shared fields of Computer Science and Cognition. I am conducting research in the intersection of Cognitive Science and Natural Language Processing. My main interests lie in cognitively inspired NLP, a field in which I endeavour to pursue a PhD. Thus, I revel in analysing cognitive data, automate computations, plotting heatmaps, writing regex, and teaching machines to understand human language.

The realms I am thinking about

and beyond my thoughts

Space

Mostly terrestrial, but also stellar

Whatever we do occurs in space. That's why I deeply hope, humans will care more about the space the live in, and the other species that live there. We have just one single environment, and should treat it as such. I am always happy to to engage in environmental friendly activities.

Time

Mostly present, but also future

What has always fascinated me most about time is the impossibility of determining it. There is no exact time. A yet unsolved scientific mistery is the incredibly subjective nature of time perception. I am really keen to investigate this matter more thoroughly. Hit me up, if you happen to be excited too, and are working on a project that's related.

Brains

Mostly frontal, but also temporal

The human brain has caught my attention ever since I was wondering about the diversity of human behaviour. Nothing, in my view, is more exciting than investigating this complex organ. I am mostly interested in the cognitive and computational side of the neurosciences.

Machines

Mostly maths, but also language

What I've always incredibly enjoyed is solving conundrums. When teaching machines to learn (whatever that may be), it's nothing more or less than trying to solve a conundrum. I mostly revel in teaching machines to understand language. Perhaps, because I really like reading and writing myself.
