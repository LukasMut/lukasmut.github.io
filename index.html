MSc student in IT and Cognition

Cognitive Neuroscience and Natural Language Processing

I am a MSc student in the shared fields of Computer Science and Cognition at the University of Copenhagen. I conduct research at the intersection of Cognitive Neuroscience and Natural Language Processing under the supervision of Isabelle Augenstein, Johannes Bjerva, and Maria Barrett . Alongside my studies, I work as a Research Assistant for Thomas T. Hildebrandt. Previously, I was an undergraduate student in Psychology at the University of Vienna. There, I worked as a Student Research Assistant in the field of Cognitive Psychology in the groups of Ulrich Ansorge and Claus Lamm, mostly supervised by Thomas Ditye. During this time, I've interned in the Clinical Neuropsychology research group at LMU Munich. I also have an undergraduate degree in Political Science & Philosophy.

My main interests lie in cognitively inspired NLP. Thus, I revel in building neural models, analysing cognitive or linguistic data, and teaching machines to read and translate. Mostly, however, I am debugging, deciphering model behaviour, using print statements, and reflecting on why things are not working. Have a look at my CV, the projects or publication section for more information about my work and interests.
